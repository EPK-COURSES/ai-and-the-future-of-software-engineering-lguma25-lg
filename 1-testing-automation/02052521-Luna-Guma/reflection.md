# Personal Reflection

Before researching this topic, I mainly associated AI with code generation tools such as ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot. I was surprised to learn that AI is also being widely used for testing, debugging, deployment automation, and software maintenance.

The most interesting part for me was AI-powered bug detection. The idea that a system can analyze large amounts of code and identify potential issues before software is released demonstrates how much software engineering is changing. I found it impressive that AI can help developers detect vulnerabilities and improve code quality much faster than traditional methods.

At the same time, one concern I have is that developers may become too dependent on AI. If programmers stop understanding the code they write and simply accept AI suggestions without verification, software quality and security could suffer. AI can make mistakes, and those mistakes may not always be obvious.

After completing this research, my opinion is that AI will not replace software engineers. Instead, it will change their responsibilities. Tasks such as repetitive testing and debugging may become more automated, while critical thinking, system design, communication, and decision-making become even more important.

As a future software engineer, I believe it is important to learn how to use AI effectively while continuing to develop strong technical skills. AI should be treated as a tool that improves productivity rather than a replacement for human expertise. The engineers who can successfully combine AI assistance with strong problem-solving abilities will likely be the most successful in the future.
